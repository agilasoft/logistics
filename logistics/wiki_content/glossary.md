# Glossary – Logistics Industry Terms

Common terms used in CargoNext and the logistics industry.

## A

**AWB (Air Waybill)** – Document for air cargo; contract between shipper and carrier.

**ABC Analysis** – Inventory classification (A = high value, B = medium, C = low) for prioritization.

## B

**Bill of Lading (BL)** – Document of title for sea freight; evidence of contract of carriage.

## C

**Chargeable Weight** – Weight used for freight pricing; max of gross and volumetric weight.

**Consignee** – Party to whom cargo is delivered.

**Container Freight Station (CFS)** – Facility for stuffing/stripping LCL containers.

**Container Yard (CY)** – Facility for full container handling.

**Customs Broker** – Licensed agent for customs clearance.

## D

**Demurrage** – Charge for container held at port beyond free time.

**Detention** – Charge for container held by customer beyond free time.

**DG (Dangerous Goods)** – Cargo requiring special handling (IATA/IMDG).

**Duty** – Tax levied on imports/exports by customs.

## E

**ETD (Estimated Time of Departure)** – Expected departure date.

**ETA (Estimated Time of Arrival)** – Expected arrival date.

**Ex Works (EXW)** – Incoterm; seller makes goods available at their premises.

## F

**FCL (Full Container Load)** – Cargo filling one or more containers.

**Freight Forwarder** – Intermediary arranging transport for shippers.

**FOB (Free on Board)** – Incoterm; seller delivers on board vessel.

## H

**House BL** – Bill of lading issued by forwarder to shipper (B2B).

**HS Code** – Harmonized System code for commodity classification.

## I

**Incoterms** – International commercial terms (FOB, CIF, DDP, etc.).

**IATA** – International Air Transport Association; air cargo standards.

## L

**LCL (Less than Container Load)** – Consolidated cargo; multiple shippers per container.

**LTL (Less Than Truckload)** – Partial truckload; multiple shippers per truck.

## M

**Master BL** – Bill of lading issued by carrier to consolidator.

**Manifest** – List of cargo for customs/carrier.

## P

**POD (Proof of Delivery)** – Evidence that cargo was delivered (signature, photo).

**Putaway** – Moving received goods to storage locations.

## S

**Shipper** – Party who tenders cargo for transport.

**TEU (Twenty-foot Equivalent Unit)** – Standard container measure (1×20ft = 1 TEU).

## U

**ULD (Unit Load Device)** – Pallet or container for aircraft loading.

**UNLOCO** – UN/LOCODE; UN location codes for ports, terminals.

## V

**VAS (Value-Added Services)** – Services beyond basic storage (labeling, kitting, etc.).

**Volumetric Weight** – Volume-based weight for air freight (L×W×H/6000).
